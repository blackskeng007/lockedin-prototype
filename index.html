ANA 204 · Page 34 of 58

The Thoracic Cavity

The thoracic cavity is bounded by the thoracic wall and the diaphragm inferiorly. It contains three principal compartments: two pleural cavities laterally and the mediastinum centrally.

The mediastinum is subdivided into superior and inferior parts; the inferior mediastinum is further divided into anterior, middle, and posterior regions, with the heart occupying the middle mediastinum.

Fig 3.4 — Mediastinal divisions

Each pleural cavity surrounds a lung and is lined by serous pleura, reducing friction during respiratory movements.
